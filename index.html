Matthew Lisondra

I am currently a Researcher of Robotics at Toronto Metropolitan University.

I do research at the Robotics and Computer Vision Laboratory (RCVL) directed by Prof. Sajad Saeedi, working on Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), State Estimation, Visual-Inertial Sensor Fusion (VIO), Computational Imaging, Computer Vision, Autonomous/Intelligent Systems Algorithms and next-generation vision sensors such as the Focal-Plane Sensor-Processor (FPSP) Chips.

I also do research at the Haptics and Telerobotics Laboratory (HapTel) directed by Prof. Kourosh Zareinia, working on Image-Based Force Estimation in Medical Applications research.

As well, I am working at Rosor Exploration, on the team doing research and development of Rosor's Active Terrain Following (ATR) system. Rosor is a near-surface geoscientific drone inspection company, specializing in remotely piloted aircraft systems development and operations to carry out mineral exploration projects and investigate for new potential mining sites.
